Chemical Composition Data Sources

The compounds in this database are based on detailed chemical composition analyses from scientific literature, including:

  • GCMS Analysis: Gas chromatography-mass spectrometry data from peer-reviewed studies identifying volatile compounds in spices and foods
  • Essential Oil Compositions: Comprehensive analyses of essential oil constituents from various plant sources
  • Food Chemistry Studies: Detailed characterizations of flavor compounds in processed foods and natural products
  • Quantitative Data: Concentration data showing relative abundances and absolute amounts of compounds in various matrices

For cinnamon specifically, GCMS analyses typically identify 20-50+ volatile compounds, with cinnamaldehyde being the major component (60-90%), followed by eugenol, linalool, cinnamyl acetate, and numerous minor constituents including terpenes, aldehydes, alcohols, and esters. The specific composition varies significantly between cinnamon species (Ceylon vs. Cassia) and processing methods.

Similar detailed composition data exists for other spices including black pepper, cloves, nutmeg, ginger, turmeric, and many others. Each spice contains dozens to hundreds of volatile compounds, many of which have documented biological activities.

About This Database

This database focuses on functional flavor compounds found in natural foods, particularly those present in the cinnamon roll example. Each compound entry includes:

  • Chemical properties and structure
  • Documented effects on human physiology
  • Mechanisms of action
  • Metabolism and bioavailability
  • Safety considerations
  • Scientific references

The information is based on peer-reviewed scientific literature and is intended for educational and research purposes.
